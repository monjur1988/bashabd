Mirpur vs Mohammadpur vs Uttara: Where Should You Rent in Dhaka? (2026)

Most "best areas in Dhaka" articles just list neighborhoods and move on. This one actually compares the three most popular affordable areas — Mirpur, Mohammadpur, and Uttara — side by side, so you can decide which fits your budget, your commute, and the life you want before you waste a single trip flat-hunting.

All three are favorites with middle-income families and young professionals, and all three are far more affordable than Gulshan, Banani, or Baridhara. But they're genuinely different places. Here's the honest breakdown.

Mirpur: best value, if you can handle the density

Mirpur is one of the most practical choices in Dhaka, especially for students, bachelors, and anyone on a tight budget. It's one of the oldest and most developed parts of the city, so you get markets, schools, hospitals, restaurants, and shopping all within reach — with no need to fight cross-city traffic for daily needs.

The good: Excellent value for money. A large, mixed population means landlords here are used to renting to all kinds of tenants, including bachelors, and the range of budgets is wide. Public transport connectivity is strong, and the Metro Rail has made getting around noticeably easier.

The trade-off: It's dense and crowded, and traffic can be heavy. It has a more working-class, busy character than the quieter planned areas. If you want calm and open space, Mirpur isn't it.

Rent in Mirpur spans a wide range depending on size and building — from modest budget flats up through more comfortable mid-range options. It remains one of the more affordable areas in the city.

Choose Mirpur if: you want the best value, don't mind crowds and noise, and want everything close by.

Mohammadpur: central, mixed, and convenient

Mohammadpur is the melting pot of the three — people from all over the country live here, and it sits right beside Dhanmondi, putting you close to a lot of the city's central hubs. That central position is its biggest selling point.

The good: Affordable for how central it is. Strong access to the rest of the city, with nearby commercial hubs like Shyamoli and Adabor meaning many people can avoid long, traffic-choked commutes to work. A genuine mix of families and single tenants, with a wide range of housing.

The trade-off: Like Mirpur, it's densely populated, with fewer green or open spaces than Uttara. The character is busy and urban rather than calm.

Rent in Mohammadpur is generally affordable and comparable to Mirpur, edging slightly up in some pockets thanks to its central location and convenience.

Choose Mohammadpur if: you want to be central and well-connected without paying premium-area prices, and you value cutting your commute.

Uttara: space and calm, at the cost of distance

Uttara is the outlier — a planned, suburban area with wide roads, organized sectors, and a more spacious, peaceful feel than the dense inner-city neighborhoods. It's a favorite among families, expats, and professionals who'll trade a longer commute for room to breathe.

The good: Organized layout, more space, schools and shopping malls, and proximity to the airport (great if you travel often). Quieter and greener than Mirpur or Mohammadpur. The Metro Rail has improved its connection to the rest of the city.

The trade-off: It's farther from the city center, so if your work or life is concentrated in central Dhaka, expect a longer daily commute. Rents at the upper end can climb, and the cost of living overall tends to run a bit higher than the inner-city affordable areas.

Rent in Uttara spans a broad range — from accessible smaller flats up to larger, well-furnished apartments that cost considerably more. You're often paying for space and calm rather than central location.

Choose Uttara if: you have a family or want peace and space, don't need to be in the city center daily, and value a planned, organized environment.

So which should you pick?

  • Tight budget, want everything nearby, okay with crowds? → Mirpur
  • Want central access and a short commute without premium prices? → Mohammadpur
  • Family or peace-seeker who can handle the distance? → Uttara
  • Bachelor or student? → Mirpur and Mohammadpur are friendlier than most; see our dedicated guide on bachelor flat rent in Dhaka.

There's no single "best" area — only the best fit for your budget, your commute, and the life you want day to day. Pick the one whose trade-off you can live with happily.
